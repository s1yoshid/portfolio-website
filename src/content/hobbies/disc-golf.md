During COVID, my dad introduced me to disc golf as a way to get my family and me out to the park for exercise while still keeping our distance from others. At first, I thought it was a little silly and was even embarrassed to play with other parkgoers watching. Six years later, though, I’m still hooked.

## So what is it?

The goal of the game is to get a disc into a basket. And yes, it’s a disc, not a frisbee — get that wrong and you’ll piss off every disc golfer in the park. The basket is made of chains and sits off the ground, and it can be set at different heights. The chains help catch the disc so it doesn’t bounce back out. The rest of the game is very similar to traditional golf: scoring, teeing off, and throwing again from where your disc lands.

## Fun to learn, hard to master

What makes the game especially interesting is that, like golf, you need to use different types of discs to play well. You have putters, midranges, drivers, distance drivers, and more. Almost every disc you buy has a slightly different flight path or feel, so you have to figure out which ones work best for you. That’s why you’ll often see players carrying huge bags full of discs. Even then, discs change over time as they get broken in, which affects how they fly.

There are also different throwing styles, like hyzer, anhyzer, and roller. It’s a fun way to spend time outside and walk around the park, but if you want to really improve, there’s a lot of depth to the sport.
